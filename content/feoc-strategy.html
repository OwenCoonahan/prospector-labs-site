The Problem

Everyone says they're FEOC compliant.
Almost nobody can prove it.

The OBBBA expanded FEOC rules to every major clean energy tax credit starting January 1, 2026. There is no government database, no standard certificate, and no verification process.

$50-150K

Per-deal legal cost

Law firms charge $50-150K per project for manual FEOC supply chain diligence. Every deal requires fresh analysis across corporate registries, import records, and supplier questionnaires.

6-12 weeks

Diligence timeline

Manual FEOC analysis takes 6-12 weeks per project. In a market where deal timelines are compressing, this is a bottleneck that kills transactions.

Self-certification is the status quo

Manufacturers fill out questionnaires with whatever answers get them the sale. There is no independent verification, no audit trail, and no accountability when attestations turn out to be wrong.

10-year recapture risk

If a manufacturer's ownership changes and becomes FEOC-tainted during the 10-year recapture period, the developer may owe back the entire credit. Ongoing monitoring doesn't exist today.

MACR Thresholds

Material Assistance Cost Ratio

Minimum percentage of project costs that must come from non-PFE sources. Miss the threshold and the entire credit is disqualified.

Data Sourcing

What's public vs. what needs outreach

Phase 1 is entirely desk research. Outreach starts in Phase 2 once we know which manufacturers actually come up in deals.

✓ Publicly Available

  • SEC filings, 10-K, proxy statements (ownership %)
  • Corporate registries (OpenCorporates, state SOS)
  • UFLPA Entity List (Customs & Border Protection)
  • DOD Section 1260H list (Chinese military companies)
  • NDAA restricted entity list
  • Import records (Panjiva/ImportGenius) for volumes & customers
  • EIA manufacturer certifications
  • Patent and IP filings (technology origin)
  • Trade association memberships and press releases
  • LinkedIn org charts and C-suite nationalities

● Requires Active Sourcing

  • Actual MACR calculations and cost breakdowns
  • Tier 2 and Tier 3 supplier relationships
  • Ownership below public disclosure thresholds
  • Existing compliance certificates and attestations
  • Inventory availability and lead times
  • Licensing agreements (effective control provisions)
  • Internal compliance documentation
  • Sub-component country of origin certificates

Competitive Landscape

No direct competitor exists

Adjacent players have pieces of the puzzle. Nobody has assembled them into a verified compliance database.

Panjiva / ImportGenius

Import records showing actual shipments, volumes, and US customers. Useful for verifying manufacturer claims about US presence.

Gap: No compliance assessment, no ownership data, no FEOC analysis

Sayari Analytics

Corporate ownership graphs and beneficial ownership intelligence. Strong on entity resolution across jurisdictions.

Gap: Not energy-specific, no MACR calculations, no component origin

Crux Climate

Tax credit marketplace. References FEOC in deal context but doesn't provide supplier-level compliance data.

Gap: Marketplace not data provider, no manufacturer profiles

Law Firms (Norton Rose, Bracewell)

Doing manual FEOC diligence on every deal. High quality but $50-150K per engagement and 6-12 week timelines.

Gap: Not scalable, not a product. Potential partners, not competitors.
